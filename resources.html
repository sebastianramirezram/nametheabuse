Urgent Support

If you are in immediate danger, contact emergency services now.

If you are in the United States or Canada, call 911. If you are elsewhere, use your local emergency number or ask a trusted person to help you find it immediately.

Talk To Someone Now

Official support lines and organizations

Relationship Abuse

National Domestic Violence Hotline

Free, confidential support in the U.S. by phone, chat, or text.

Call: 800-799-SAFE (7233)

Text: START to 88788

thehotline.org
Sexual Violence

RAINN National Sexual Assault Hotline

Confidential support in English and Spanish via phone, chat, or text.

Call: 800-656-HOPE (4673)

Text: HOPE to 64673

rainn.org
Mental Health Crisis

988 Lifeline

Judgment-free crisis support in the U.S. by call, text, or chat.

Call or text: 988

988lifeline.org

Digital Safety

If someone may be monitoring your device, browsing history, location, or messages, try using a safer device when possible. The National Domestic Violence Hotline also offers digital safety guidance through its site.

A Note

You do not need to have the “right” language before contacting support. Confusion, uncertainty, and mixed feelings are all valid reasons to reach out.

If You Are Helping Someone Else

Try to listen without rushing to solve everything at once.

Offer practical support: help finding a hotline, a ride, a safer device, or a private moment to talk.

Avoid pressing for certainty. It is often more helpful to say "I believe you" or "we can look at options together."

How To Use This Page

You can use this page in different ways: as a crisis reference, as a starting point for someone you care about, or as a slower resource page to return to after reading other sections of the site. It is here to reduce friction when clarity or support is needed most.

International Support

Country-specific support beyond the United States

Support systems vary by country. Some have one national service, while others rely on provincial directories or city-based response lines. The resources below are official starting points with country-specific contact details.

Canada

ShelterSafe

A national shelter directory and crisis-entry resource run by Women's Shelters Canada. It links to nearby shelters and province-wide help lines.

Emergency: 911 in immediate danger

Example crisis lines: Alberta 780-310-1818, BC 1-800-563-0808

sheltersafe.ca
United Kingdom

National Domestic Abuse Help

The U.K. government’s domestic abuse page links to national and regional helplines for England, Scotland, and Northern Ireland, plus emergency calling guidance.

England: 0808 2000 247

Emergency: 999

gov.uk
Australia

1800RESPECT

Australia’s national domestic, family and sexual violence counselling, information, and support service.

Call: 1800 737 732

Text: 0458 737 732

Emergency: 000

1800respect.org.au
Mexico

SOS Mujeres / Línea Mujeres

Mexico does not use one single national abuse hotline in the same way as some other countries. A strong official starting point is the Mexico City women’s support system, alongside local emergency services.

Emergency: 911

SOS Mujeres: *765

Línea Mujeres: 55 5658 1111

mujeresseguras.cdmx.gob.mx
Colombia

Línea 155

A free, 24-hour national orientation line for women experiencing violence, promoted by Colombia’s National Police. It offers guidance on rights, reporting, and where to seek legal and health support.

Call: 155

Emergency: 123

policia.gov.co
China

12338 Women's Rights Service

China commonly routes women’s rights and domestic violence support through the Women’s Federation 12338 rights-service network. Coverage is often organized at the local or provincial level, but 12338 is the core number readers should look for.

Women's rights line: 12338

Emergency: 110

women.wuxi.gov.cn
Brazil

Ligue 180

Brazil’s Ministry of Women runs Ligue 180 as a 24/7 national service for guidance, complaints, and referrals for violence against women. It can also be reached by WhatsApp.

Call: 180

WhatsApp: (61) 9610-0180

Emergency: 190

gov.br
